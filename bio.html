Summer 2023 Got NSF funding to do PARADIM-REU at Johns Hopkins University. Worked w/ Dr. Tyrel McQueen on computational methods for materials discovery. Learned about density-functional theory (DFT), ML, and molecular dynamics. Some key results:
  • Trained a machine learning model to predict a material's critical temperature, oxidation suspectibility, and percentage of removable oxides from it's elemental composition. Said dataset consisted of ~170k entries (17k from SuperCon, 150k from Materials Project). Model training was done with the XGBoost algorithm, Nelder-Mead method, and Convex Hull optimization. This model was run on the ARCH Rockfish supercluster, and I made a Python interface to predict these properties for new materials.
  • Tried (and failed) to simulate Tantalum oxidation using LAMMPS. Unfortunately, oxygen molecules getting hotter than the sun is not ideal for realistic simulations.
  • Presented my work at student conferences at Cornell and Hopkins at end of the summer. Presentation, Poster and Github
